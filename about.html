About Us

Our story, mission, and the people behind the pages

Our Story

From a single phone booth to a community-wide literacy movement.

The Second Chapter began in 2021 when two former librarians, Sarah Nkosi and David Chen, converted an abandoned phone booth in Braamfontein into a tiny street library. What started as a small experiment quickly grew into a movement. Neighbours donated books, children gathered to read, and within months, the phone booth couldn't hold all the love — and books — pouring in.

Today, we operate two full community hubs and a mobile library service, reaching over 5,000 readers annually. We believe that every book holds the power to start a new chapter in someone's life.

Our Mission

To make reading accessible to every child and adult by creating free community book exchanges and literacy programmes in under-resourced South African communities.

Our Vision

A South Africa where no community is a book desert, and every person has access to stories that inspire, educate, and empower.
